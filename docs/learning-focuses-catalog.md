# Early-learning focus catalogue

## Product language by age

For birth through age five, RummageLab uses **developmental focus** language:
“designed to support noticing sounds” or “invites turn-taking practice.” It does
not claim that a child has achieved a milestone, mastered a skill, or met a
formal standard.

For ages 5–6, carefully reviewed kindergarten standards may be introduced as an
optional layer. They should not be used to evaluate a child automatically.

## Internal MVP tags

These are RummageLab’s internal tags, informed by early-learning practice; they
are not official ELOF identifiers or assessments.

| ID | Focus | Kitchen Sound Detectives example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `DEV.ATL.CURIOSITY` | Curiosity and flexible exploration | Try a different surface after a surprising sound |
| `DEV.LANG.DESCRIPTIVE_WORDS` | Descriptive language | Use loud, quiet, fast, slow, same, or different |
| `DEV.COG.CAUSE_EFFECT` | Cause and effect | Notice that materials change a sound |
| `DEV.COG.PATTERN` | Early patterning | Copy or extend a two-beat rhythm |
| `DEV.SOC.TURN_TAKING` | Shared attention and turn-taking | Take turns making and copying a sound |
| `DEV.PMP.CONTROLLED_MOVEMENT` | Controlled movement | Gently tap with a spoon or spatula |

## Age-stage guidance

| Stage | Experience mode | Length | Product stance |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 0–12 months | `caregiver_moment` | 2–5 min | Parent narrates sensory contrast; no child device use |
| 12–36 months | `co_play` | 3–8 min | Parent and child use curated large objects together |
| 3–4 years | `guided_quest` | 8–12 min | Picture/audio-led prompts, simple prediction and noticing |
| 4–6 years | `guided_quest` | 10–15 min | Prediction, testing, making, explaining; optional reviewed K links |

## Reference

The [Head Start Early Learning Outcomes Framework](https://headstart.gov/school-readiness/article/head-start-early-learning-outcomes-framework)
is a useful reference for birth-to-five developmental domains. It should guide
human curation, not be represented as automated assessment.
